The seven continents of the Earth are Asia, Africa, North America, South America, Antarctica, Europe, and Australia, with Asia being the largest and Australia the smallest; these large landmasses are differentiated by geographical boundaries and are considered distinct regions with unique ecosystems and cultural characteristics, often separated by vast oceans, and are believed to have once been part of a single supercontinent called Pangaea which gradually drifted apart over millions of years.
